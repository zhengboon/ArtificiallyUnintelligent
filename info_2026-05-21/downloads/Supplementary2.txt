 TITLE

        telling where it is safe to go. This is very reactive.

 The "Virtual Target" Approach
 TITLE

   SUBTITLE

   · Let's say there is a list of positions that the drone
        should visit. Instead of sending goal positions to the
        drone, send Virtual Targets. The virtual target is the
        result of vectors:
          · Step 1: Calculate the Goal Vector (the direction
               to the the target positions from current
               position).

          · Step 2: Calculate the Avoidance Vector (the
               direction away from obstacles or positions to
               avoid the obstacles).

          · Step 3: Sum them to get the Resultant
               positions.

          · Step 4: Project a point a short distance along
               that resultant direction. This point is your
               set_point for the next movement.

   · The distance of the Look-Ahead (d) affects alot:
          · If d is too large: The drone may try to cut
               corners through obstacles because the target
               is on the other side.
          · If d is too small: The drone will move very slowly
               and jitter, as the position controller reaches
               the set-point too quickly.

 Obstacle-Free Polar Mapping
 TITLE

   SUBTITLE

   · The drone scans its surroundings as a 360° map of
        distances. Then:
          · Step A: The Depth Histogram. The drone
               divides its surroundings into "bins" (e.g., every
               10°) as it yaw quarter by quarter.
          · Step B: The Mask. It marks bins as Blocked (if
               an obstacle is closer than a safety threshold) or
               Free.
          · Step C: The Selection. It looks at all the Free
               bins and chooses the one that has the smallest
               angular difference from the Goal Vector.

 Object Detection using Yolo
 TITLE

   SUBTITLE

   · Qualifier requires your code to detect specific object
        to earn points.

   · Object detection allows the quadcopter to not only
        "see" an obstacle but to identify exactly what it is,
        such as a person, a wall, or another drone.

   · By using the YOLO (You Only Look Once) algorithm,
        the system can detect multiple objects in real-time
        with high accuracy, enabling the drone to make
        split-second navigation decisions.

   · We utilize YOLO for object detection because it
        treats detection as a single regression problem,
        processing the entire image in one pass to predict
        bounding boxes and class probabilities
        simultaneously.

   · This single-shot approach is ideal for quadcopters,
        providing the low-latency inference required to
        update avoidance vectors at high speeds.

 Occupancy Grid
 TITLE

   SUBTITLE

   · As the quadcopter explores the area, it collected
        data about accessible and un-accessible grids.

   · These grid can be used to create map of the entire
        area in the form of occupancy grid which is useful to
        calculating path towards a destination.

   · Qualifier Challenge does not require team to
        generate occupancy grid maps.

   · Learning an occupancy grid is the right next step
        towards the Final Challenge.

   · But the trick is doing it consistently with your
        camera frame, NED frame, and noisy depth. Most
        failures I see aren't from the math--they're from
        frame mismatch and bad update logic.
         · The solutions to these challenges will be
               discussed in the Supplementary Materials.
